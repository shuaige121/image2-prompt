# Fashion & Beauty Editorial

> **When to use:** Pick this when the request centers on a styled human wearing or modeling clothing, makeup, hair, or accessories — magazine-style editorials, studio fashion, beauty close-ups, or apparel/e-com lookbook shots (as opposed to candid people, food, or pure product).

## Base recipe

Write it as a shot brief, in this order — scene → subject → wardrobe/beauty → lens & framing → light → film/color → texture anchors → constraint. Paste-ready skeleton:

"Photorealistic editorial fashion photograph. [SCENE/BACKDROP: e.g. seamless warm-grey studio sweep / sun-bleached concrete stairwell at golden hour]. [SUBJECT: one model, age + build + skin tone, candid weight-shift pose, looking slightly off-camera]. Wearing [WARDROBE: garment, fabric, fit — note real fabric behavior: linen creasing at the elbow, silk sliding off the shoulder]. [HAIR/MAKEUP if relevant: e.g. glossy slicked-back hair, dewy bare-skin makeup with visible texture]. Shot on an 85mm lens at f/2.8, [FRAMING: three-quarter length, eye level], shallow depth of field with the background falling soft. [LIGHT: large softbox key from camera-left at 45 degrees + white fill, soft shadow under the jaw / window light raking across one cheek]. [FILM/COLOR: Kodak Portra 400, fine grain, warm neutral skin tones / muted cool color grade]. Visible skin pores and fine peach-fuzz catching the light, a few flyaway hairs, natural specular sheen on the nose and forehead, fabric showing real weave and slight wrinkles. Unretouched, natural imperfections kept."

Rules: name ONE concrete lens + aperture, ONE light direction, ONE film/color reference. Always end with a texture-and-imperfection clause — it is the single biggest defense against the plastic-skin AI tell. Describe the pose as a verb in motion (shifting weight, mid-stride, adjusting a sleeve), never "posing." For e-com keep it flat, even, and centered; for editorial let light be directional and a little imperfect.

## Sub-categories

### Studio fashion editorial (seamless / colorama)
- **When:** Magazine or campaign look on a clean studio backdrop, full styling, dramatic or sculpted light.
- **Camera:** 85mm at f/4-f/8, three-quarter or full length, eye level
- **Lighting:** 22-26in beauty dish key (semi-hard, crisp catchlight) + small softbox or reflector fill below = clamshell; or single beauty dish for sculpted shadow

```text
Photorealistic studio fashion editorial. One model in a structured wool coat, mid-stride toward camera, weight on the back foot. Shot on an 85mm lens at f/4, three-quarter length, eye level against a seamless warm-grey sweep. 24-inch beauty dish key from above camera-left as the main light with a small softbox fill below, crisp catchlights and a defined soft shadow under the jaw. Neutral high-contrast color, fine grain. Visible skin pores, fine peach-fuzz on the cheek, natural sheen on the forehead, wool showing real nap and a few stray fibers.
```


### On-location editorial (street / natural light)
- **When:** Story-driven editorial in a real environment — street, interior, landscape — natural or window light, cinematic film look.
- **Camera:** 35mm or 50mm at f/2.0-f/2.8, full or three-quarter length, eye level
- **Lighting:** Golden-hour backlight + open shade fill, or soft overcast daylight; window light raking one cheek for interiors

```text
Photorealistic on-location fashion editorial. One model in a flowing slip dress leaning against a sun-bleached stucco wall, looking off-frame, hair lifting slightly in the breeze. Shot on a 50mm lens at f/2.0, full length, eye level, late-afternoon golden-hour backlight wrapping the shoulders with a warm rim, soft open shade on the face. Kodak Portra 400, fine grain, warm peachy skin tones, gentle low contrast. Visible pores and flyaway hairs catching the sun, fabric translucent where the light passes through, natural skin sheen, slight motion in the hem.
```


### Beauty close-up (skin, makeup, hair)
- **When:** Tight crop selling makeup, skin, brows, lips, or hair detail; cosmetics/skincare context.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro at f/8-f/11, head-on, forehead-to-chin crop
- **Lighting:** Ring light for even fill + ring catchlight; or clamshell (softbox above + reflector below) for soft wraparound

```text
Photorealistic beauty close-up. Tight crop on a model's face from forehead to chin, glossy editorial makeup, glowing dewy skin, lips slightly parted. Shot on a 100mm macro lens at f/8, head-on, even ring-light illumination producing a circular catchlight in each iris with a small softbox fill. Neutral clean color, true-to-life tones. Sharp visible pores with organic size variation across the nose and cheeks, fine vellus peach-fuzz along the jaw and upper lip, real specular variation between the oily T-zone and matte cheeks, individual brow hairs and lash separation, no smoothing.
```


### Lookbook / e-commerce apparel (on-model)
- **When:** Catalog-style full outfit on a model for a brand lookbook or PDP; clean, consistent, garment-forward.
- **Camera:** 50mm at f/8, full length feet-in, eye level, centered
- **Lighting:** Two softboxes at 45 degrees both sides, even and shadow-free; small floor contact shadow

```text
Photorealistic e-commerce fashion lookbook photograph. One model standing relaxed and front-facing in a tailored blazer and trousers, full body with feet included, arms slightly away from the torso so the garment silhouette reads clearly. Shot on a 50mm lens at f/8, full length, eye level against a clean white seamless backdrop. Two softboxes at 45 degrees both sides for even shadow-free light, soft floor contact shadow. Neutral accurate color, true garment tones. Visible fabric weave, real seams and stitching, natural drape and a few honest wrinkles, fine skin texture and pores, matte natural skin.
```


### Ghost-mannequin / flat-lay product apparel
- **When:** Garment-only shot with no visible model — invisible-mannequin 3D hollow effect or top-down flat lay for marketplace listings.
- **Camera:** 50mm at f/11, centered, eye level (top-down for flat lay)
- **Lighting:** Two softboxes at 45 degrees, flat even fill, white background for clean cutout

```text
Photorealistic ghost-mannequin apparel product shot. A navy crewneck sweater shown as if worn by an invisible body, hollow neckline and cuffs revealing the inner collar and label, dimensional 3D form. Shot on a 50mm lens at f/11, centered, eye level on a pure white seamless background. Two softboxes at 45 degrees for even diffuse light, no harsh shadows. Accurate color, true fabric tone. Visible knit texture and ribbing, real seams and stitching, natural drape at the shoulders, slight fabric pilling, no model.
```


### High-fashion dramatic editorial (gels / hard light)
- **When:** Avant-garde or couture editorial with bold color gels, hard shadow, theatrical mood — Vogue/i-D energy.
- **Camera:** 85mm at f/5.6, three-quarter length, low or eye level
- **Lighting:** Single hard bare bulb / fresnel key for crisp shadow + colored gel rim/back light for separation

```text
Photorealistic avant-garde fashion editorial. One model in a sculptural couture gown, angular pose with a turned shoulder and lifted chin, looking past the lens. Shot on an 85mm lens at f/5.6, three-quarter length, slightly low angle against deep shadow. Single bare hard light from high camera-right casting a crisp dark shadow across the wall, a magenta gel rim from behind separating the figure. Saturated cinematic color, fine grain. Visible skin pores and sheen on the lit cheek, sharp shadow edge, fabric catching hard specular highlights, a few flyaway hairs in the rim light.
```

## img2img / reference-image

Use a reference image whenever identity, garment, or pose must stay consistent across a set. Pass the product/garment as the reference and prompt only the changes: "keep the garment, fabric, color, and print exactly; restyle the scene/light only." For virtual try-on, supply garment + model separately and describe the drape ("the dress falls naturally over the shoulders, fabric follows the body"). For lookbook consistency, lock the model reference and vary only wardrobe/background so faces match across shots. When fixing plastic skin on an AI output, run an edit pass: "add visible pores and fine micro-texture, keep natural fine lines and peach-fuzz, no smoothing, no AI glow." For e-com, reference a real product photo so true color and stitching transfer rather than being invented. gpt-image-2 interprets camera specs loosely — use lens/aperture for the look, and lean on the reference image plus the texture clause to actually carry realism.
