# Macro / Close-Up (food, product, nature, texture)

> **When to use:** Pick this when the subject fills the frame at extreme close range and the point IS the micro-texture — crumb, dewdrop, gem facet, fabric weave, juice beads, pollen — not the whole object or scene.

## Base recipe

Extreme close-up photograph of [SUBJECT], shot on a 100mm macro lens at near 1:1 life-size magnification, [APERTURE: f/2.8 for a single razor-thin plane of focus / f/8 focus-stacked look for edge-to-edge sharpness]. Frame so the subject fills the frame; razor-thin plane of focus on [EXACT FOCUS POINT], everything in front and behind melting into smooth creamy bokeh. [LIGHTING: e.g. soft diffused window light from camera left, single specular highlight running along the wet edge]. Real micro-texture in sharp focus: [3-4 concrete texture anchors — pores, fibers, condensation beads, sugar crystals, scratches, pollen grains]. Natural color, true-to-life, subtle imperfections and a few stray particles. Honest unretouched close-up, not a glossy render. Photorealistic.

## Sub-categories

### Food macro — crumb, steam, pour
- **When:** Bread/cake cross-section, melting cheese, coffee crema, frosting swirl, sizzling meat, honey drizzle — the appetite is in the texture.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/2.8–f/4 for thin focus; raking side-back light is the food-macro signature
- **Lighting:** hard-ish side light + backlight for rim + visible steam against a dark ground

```text
Extreme close-up on a 100mm macro lens, near 1:1, f/3.2 razor-thin focus on the [torn crumb / cheese pull / crema surface]. Side-back light from camera right rakes across the surface to reveal texture, with a bright rim highlight on the wet/glossy edge and steam wisps catching the light against a darker background. Sharp micro-detail: open irregular crumb holes, glistening melted-butter sheen, tiny sugar crystals, a few stray crumbs. Warm natural tones, real moisture, creamy out-of-focus background. Not over-styled.
```


### Food macro — liquid & droplet
- **When:** Pour shots, droplet on fruit, condensation on a cold glass, splash, oil beads, juice running.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/4; frontmost droplet on the focal plane
- **Lighting:** backlight through droplets so they refract and glow; one catchlight per bead

```text
Macro close-up of [water droplets on a strawberry / condensation beading on a cold glass], 100mm macro near 1:1, f/4, focus locked on the frontmost droplet. Backlight rakes through the droplets so each bead glows and acts as a tiny lens; razor-thin focus, the rest dissolving to bokeh. Crisp micro-detail: surface pores and seeds visible through the refracting droplets, a single specular catchlight in each bead, faint surface fuzz. Cool fresh tones, real wetness, a couple of irregular running drips. Honest, un-retouched.
```


### Product macro — jewelry & gemstone
- **When:** Ring, diamond facets, watch dial/movement, engraving, clasp detail — showing precision and 'fire'.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/8 focus-stacked sharpness; tripod-locked
- **Lighting:** diffused light-tent base + one hard 45° spot for sparkle; control reflections

```text
Macro product close-up of [a diamond solitaire ring], 100mm macro near 1:1, focus-stacked look at f/8 for edge-to-edge sharpness across the facets. Soft diffused light-tent base lighting to control reflections, plus one small undiffused spot at 45 degrees from above to spark fire and pinfire scintillation in the stone. Sharp micro-detail: individual facet edges, a tiny natural inclusion, fine prong-setting marks, faint polishing lines on the metal. Clean gradient background, true metal color, restrained specular highlights — no blown-out hotspots.
```


### Product macro — cosmetics & liquid texture
- **When:** Lipstick bullet, cream swirl, powder, serum droplet, foundation swatch on skin, gel texture.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/5.6; leading glossy edge on the focal plane
- **Lighting:** soft diffused top-left + single specular curve highlight; gradient falloff

```text
Extreme close-up of [a lipstick bullet tip / a cream swatch], 100mm macro near 1:1, f/5.6, razor-thin focus on the leading edge. Soft diffused light from above-left with a gentle gradient falloff, one subtle specular highlight tracing the glossy curve. Sharp micro-detail: creamy emulsion texture, tiny shimmer flecks catching light, a clean wet sheen, fine drag marks in the swatch. Saturated-but-true product color, smooth bokeh background, a believable smear edge — not a plastic CGI render.
```


### Product macro — material & texture detail
- **When:** Leather grain, stitching, watch knurling, fabric weave, brushed metal, wood grain, label print — build-quality close-ups.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/8 stacked; flat-on or slight angle to the surface
- **Lighting:** hard low-angle raking side light to maximize relief and micro-shadow

```text
Macro detail shot of [hand-stitched leather grain / brushed-steel knurling], 100mm macro near 1:1, f/8 focus-stacked for full surface sharpness. Hard low-angle raking light from one side to throw every fiber, pore and tool mark into relief, with deep micro-shadows. Sharp micro-detail: individual thread fibers and needle holes, leather pore pattern, fine machining grooves, a subtle scuff. Neutral true-to-material color, tactile and physical, honest wear — reads as a real surface, not a tiled texture map.
```


### Nature macro — insect & dewdrop
- **When:** Bug, bee, butterfly wing, spider, frost crystals, a dew-laden web at dawn.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/2.8 razor-thin focus on the eyes; handheld field feel
- **Lighting:** soft directional dawn light; dew beads carry catchlights

```text
Nature macro of [a damselfly at dawn], 100mm macro near 1:1, f/2.8 for a razor-thin plane of focus on the compound eyes. Soft early-morning natural light, cool and directional, dewdrops along the body acting as tiny lenses. Sharp micro-detail: faceted compound eye, fine wing veins and translucency, individual leg hairs, water beads each holding a single catchlight. Background a wash of creamy green-and-gold bokeh, real out-of-focus depth. Candid field shot, slight motion in a stray hair — not a clinical studio specimen.
```


### Nature macro — flower & botanical
- **When:** Petal texture, pollen-laden stamen, leaf veins, moss, seed head, water on petals.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/4; anther/pollen on the focal plane
- **Lighting:** soft diffused overcast or gentle backlight for translucent petal glow

```text
Macro close-up of [a flower's stamen and pollen], 100mm macro near 1:1, f/4, razor-thin focus on the pollen-dusted anther. Soft diffused overcast daylight, gentle directional shaping. Sharp micro-detail: individual pollen grains, fine hairs on the filament, translucent petal cells with backlit glow, a single dew bead. Lush creamy bokeh background in natural greens, true botanical color, an asymmetric living imperfection on one petal. Honest close-up, the kind found in the field at first light.
```


### Abstract texture macro — pattern fill
- **When:** Full-frame texture as wallpaper/background: paint cracks, rust, ice, fabric, bubbles, food surface — no single 'subject'.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, ~1:1, f/8 for uniform sharpness; sensor parallel to the surface
- **Lighting:** even soft raking light across the whole plane; avoid central hotspot

```text
Full-frame abstract macro texture of [oxidized weathered copper / cracked paint / soap bubble film], 100mm macro near 1:1, f/8 for even edge-to-edge sharpness across the whole surface. Even soft raking light to bring out relief without hotspots. Fills the entire frame edge to edge, no central subject, the pattern itself is the image. Sharp micro-detail: irregular organic cracking, mineral granularity, iridescent oil-slick color shifts, fine pitting. True material color, tactile and physical, natural randomness — a real photographed surface, not a seamless generated tile.
```

## img2img / reference-image

Macro is the genre where img2img pays off most, because the model is weak at inventing physically-correct micro-texture (real crumb structure, true facet geometry, correct droplet refraction). Provide a real macro reference and label its role explicitly: 'Image 1 is the subject/texture reference — keep its exact surface texture, color and micro-detail; re-light and re-frame as an extreme close-up.' For products, pass the actual product as Image 1 ('preserve exact shape, logo and material — verbatim') and optionally a lighting/mood shot as Image 2 ('style reference only, do not copy its subject'). Use a low-to-moderate denoise/edit strength so the reference's real grain, pores and facets survive — high strength reverts to a smooth CGI look. For tiling/texture fills, a reference photo of the real material prevents the seamless-render giveaway. The model interprets aperture/lens loosely, so still write the look in plain language (razor-thin focus, creamy bokeh, raking light) rather than relying on f-numbers alone; if focus drifts to the wrong spot, name the exact focal point ('focus on the frontmost droplet') since first words carry the most weight.
