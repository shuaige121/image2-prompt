# Event & Wedding Photography

> **When to use:** Pick this when the request is a wedding or live event — ceremony candids, reception/dance floor, posed couple or group portraits, or styled detail flat-lays — where real moments, mixed venue light, and a photojournalistic look matter more than a clean studio render.

## Base recipe

A real wedding photograph, [SUBCATEGORY SNIPPET]. Shot on a full-frame mirrorless body with a [LENS, e.g. 35mm f/1.4 / 85mm f/1.4 / 100mm macro] prime, [APERTURE], [shutter/motion cue if any]. [LIGHTING: name the real source and its direction — window light from camera-left, low backlit sun, candle/tungsten venue ambience, on-camera bounce flash]. Natural unposed body language and genuine expression, caught mid-moment. Real skin with visible pores and slight flush, flyaway hairs, fabric that creases and wrinkles, a few specular highlights on metal and glass. Color and grain of Kodak Portra 400, warm neutral skin tones, gentle highlight roll-off, slightly muted shadows, fine film grain. Available-light exposure with believable falloff into the background, shallow depth of field, soft out-of-focus background. Candid documentary framing, slightly imperfect — not centered, not retouched. [ASPECT RATIO].

## Sub-categories

### Ceremony candid (vows / first kiss / reactions)
- **When:** User wants the emotional unrepeatable moment at the altar — vows, ring exchange, first kiss, a guest's tears or laughter.
- **Camera:** 85mm f/1.4 prime at f/2, 1/500s, shot from the back of the aisle for compression and distance
- **Lighting:** soft directional window/ambient light, no flash, available light only

```text
a candid ceremony moment at the altar, the couple mid-vow with a tear catching the light, guests softly blurred in the pews behind. Shot on an 85mm f/1.4 prime at f/2, 1/500s to freeze the moment, from the back of the aisle so nothing is interrupted. Soft directional window light through tall church or venue windows, no flash, available light only. Compressed background, shallow focus on the couple's faces, real watery eyes and a trembling lip, slight motion in a turning head. Portra 400 color, warm skin, fine grain. Reportage framing, caught not posed.
```


### Aisle / processional wide
- **When:** User wants the full scene — the walk down the aisle, the venue, guests' faces, the whole room reacting.
- **Camera:** 35mm f/1.4 prime at f/2.8, eye level from the aisle for an environmental documentary wide
- **Lighting:** available daylight or warm string-light venue ambience, no flash

```text
the bride walking down the aisle, full venue visible — arches, florals, rows of guests turning to look. Shot on a 35mm f/1.4 prime at f/2.8 for context and depth, eye level from the aisle. Available daylight or warm string-light ambience filling the room, gentle falloff toward the back. Documentary wide framing that shows the place and the faces, real candid reactions, a few raised phones among guests. Portra 400 color, warm neutral tones, fine grain, slightly off-center composition.
```


### Reception / dance floor (shutter drag + flash)
- **When:** User wants energy and party — dancing, sparklers, low-light celebration with motion and color streaks.
- **Camera:** 35mm f/1.4 at f/4, slow 1/15s shutter, ISO 800, rear-curtain sync flash
- **Lighting:** on-camera bounce/direct flash freezing the subject over slow ambient party light for motion streaks

```text
the couple dancing on a packed reception floor at night, on-camera flash freezing them crisp while colored DJ uplights and string lights smear into warm motion streaks behind. Dragged-shutter long exposure look: subject sharp, background blurred with light trails. Shot on a 35mm f/1.4 at f/4, slow 1/15s shutter, ISO 800, rear-curtain sync flash. Tungsten-and-magenta party color, deep shadows, real sweat-sheen on skin, a blurred swinging arm. Energetic candid framing, dark club-lit ambience, fine grain.
```


### Golden-hour couple portrait
- **When:** User wants the romantic editorial portrait — the couple alone in beautiful low light, posed but relaxed.
- **Camera:** 85mm f/1.4 prime wide open at f/1.6, backlit into the low sun for rim light and natural flare
- **Lighting:** low golden-hour sun directly behind subjects, organic flare, no artificial light

```text
the couple together in an open field or rooftop at sunset, low sun directly behind them throwing a rim of light around hair, veil and shoulders, organic hazy lens flare across the frame. Shot on an 85mm f/1.4 prime wide open at f/1.6, backlit. Loose romantic posing — foreheads touching, a quiet laugh — flowing fabric catching the wind. Fuji 400H pastel-and-airy color, soft contrast, glowing highlights, warm haze, fine grain. Shallow focus, dreamy bokeh, slightly underexposed-then-lifted shadows.
```


### Detail flat-lay (rings, invitation suite, florals)
- **When:** User wants the styled still-life — ring shot, invitation suite, shoes, bouquet, jewelry, perfume.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro lens, top-down flat-lay at f/8 (or f/5.6–f/8 close ring macro)
- **Lighting:** soft directional window light raking from the side for texture and dimension

```text
a styled flat-lay of the wedding details — invitation suite as the anchor placed off-center, rings, silk ribbon, a sprig of eucalyptus and loose petals layered in a loose triangle. Shot top-down on a 100mm macro lens at f/8 for sharp detail across the spread. Soft directional window light from camera-left raking across textured handmade paper and metal, gentle shadows giving dimension. Real paper grain, ink texture, tiny scratches on the gold band, dewy petals. Portra 400 color, warm neutral, fine grain. Rule-of-thirds composition, intentional but unfussy.
```


### Getting-ready / prep candid
- **When:** User wants the morning-of intimacy — hair and makeup, buttoning the dress, quiet pre-ceremony moments in the suite.
- **Camera:** 35mm f/1.4 at f/2, eye level, candid documentary distance
- **Lighting:** large soft window light as the only source, bright and airy

```text
a getting-ready moment in a bright hotel suite, bridesmaids buttoning the back of the dress while the bride looks out a large window, the gown on a hanger glowing in the light behind. Shot on a 35mm f/1.4 at f/2, eye level, candid. Big soft window light as the only source, airy and clean, gentle falloff into the room. Real expressions mid-action, a hand mid-button, slight motion. Portra 400 color, light and warm, fine grain. Documentary framing with breathing room around the subject.
```


### Speeches & toasts (warm tungsten candid)
- **When:** User wants the reception-table emotion — a toast, laughter, the couple reacting during a speech under warm indoor light.
- **Camera:** 85mm f/1.4 at f/2, 1/200s, subtle ceiling-bounce flash
- **Lighting:** warm candle/tungsten venue ambience lifted by gentle bounce flash, ambience preserved

```text
a candid toast moment at the head table, the best man mid-speech with the couple laughing and the bride wiping a happy tear, warm candlelight and tungsten venue glow on faces. Shot on an 85mm f/1.4 at f/2, 1/200s, slight on-camera bounce flash off the ceiling to lift shadows while keeping the warm ambience. Real laughter creasing eyes, a raised champagne glass catching light, soft background of blurred guests. Portra 400 color, warm tungsten skin, fine grain. Reportage framing, caught mid-reaction.
```

## img2img / reference-image

For img2img / --ref, feed a real wedding frame in the exact target style and ask gpt-image-2 to "match the lighting direction, film color and grain, and candid framing of the reference; keep the same lens compression and depth of field" rather than copying the people. Strong reference patterns: (1) for golden-hour portraits, supply a backlit-flare reference so the model anchors the rim light and haze instead of inventing flat front light; (2) for dance-floor shots, supply a dragged-shutter/flash reference so it keeps the sharp-subject + smeared-background contrast — text alone often renders it as a clean bright frame; (3) for detail flat-lays, supply an overhead styled reference to lock the top-down angle, off-center anchor, and triangular layout, since gpt-image-2 drifts to a 3/4 angle otherwise; (4) for ceremony candids, a reference fixes the venue type (church vs barn vs ballroom) and keeps guests softly blurred rather than sharp and staring. Keep reference strength moderate so real skin texture and film grain survive; if output looks too clean/AI, lower how much you lean on the ref and re-add the texture and Portra/Fuji grain anchors in the prompt. To keep a real couple's identity across many shots, reuse one clear reference face per person and restate it each call; never let the model average toward generic "model" faces.
