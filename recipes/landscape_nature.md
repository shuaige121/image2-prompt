# Landscape & Nature

> **When to use:** Pick this when the request is an outdoor scene with no central human subject — vistas, mountains, coastlines, forests, deserts, water, skies, or minimalist nature — where the goal is depth, light, and place rather than a product or portrait.

## Base recipe

Shot on a [full-frame DSLR / Fujifilm medium format / 35mm film] with a [16-35mm wide / 24-70mm / 70-200mm telephoto] lens at [focal length]mm, [aperture f/8-f/16 for deep focus], ISO 100, tripod-mounted. [Camera position + angle: low to the foreground / eye-level / from a ridge looking down]. [Time of day + light direction: low golden-hour sun raking from frame left / overcast soft light / blue-hour afterglow]. Foreground anchor: [wet rock, grass tufts, dune ripples, fallen leaves] in sharp focus; midground [subject]; background [distant peaks / horizon] receding into [aerial haze / mist]. Natural color balance, [Fuji Velvia / Kodak Portra / muted desaturated] color science, fine grain. Real textures: [lichen on granite, bark fissures, foam streaks, frost crystals], visible atmospheric depth, true-to-life dynamic range with detail held in both shadows and highlights. Honest, unposed, slightly imperfect — not a postcard.

## Sub-categories

### Golden-hour mountain vista
- **When:** Wide grand scenery at sunrise/sunset — peaks, valleys, ridgelines with warm directional light
- **Camera:** 16-35mm wide-angle at 20-24mm, f/11-f/13 for deep focus, ISO 100, tripod, hyperfocal
- **Lighting:** Low golden-hour sun raking across the scene from one side, long warm shadows, sidelit ridges, aerial haze separating distance layers

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 16-35mm wide-angle lens at 22mm, f/11, ISO 100, tripod-mounted, low to a foreground of frost-tipped grass. Sunrise: low golden sun rakes from frame right, warm light skimming the ridgeline and casting long shadows across the valley floor. Snow-dusted peaks recede into pale blue aerial haze, separated mist between ranges. Fuji Velvia color science, saturated but natural, fine grain, detail held in both lit slopes and shadowed valley.
```


### Seascape / coastal
- **When:** Ocean, cliffs, rocky shores, tide pools, headlands — anything where water meets land
- **Camera:** 24mm wide at f/13 deep focus, or 70-200mm for compressed wave/headland layers; polarizer to cut surface glare
- **Lighting:** Side or backlit low sun, blue-hour cool tones, or moody overcast; raking light to texture wet rock and foam

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 24mm lens at f/13, ISO 100, tripod low over barnacle-crusted wet rock in the foreground. Circular polarizer cutting surface glare. Overcast blue-hour light, cool muted tones, soft even illumination. Incoming swell wraps around a black basalt headland, foam streaking across kelp-covered stone, spray haze in the air. Natural color balance, restrained contrast, salt-wet texture and visible water motion at the rock edge.
```


### Long-exposure water (silk)
- **When:** Waterfalls, streams, ocean swell, or moving cloud rendered smooth and silky by a long shutter
- **Camera:** 24-50mm at f/11-f/16, ISO 100, 6-stop ND filter, tripod; shutter several seconds to minutes
- **Lighting:** Overcast or golden-hour shade (ND filters thrive in lower light); avoid harsh midday

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 35mm lens at f/16, ISO 100, 6-stop ND filter, tripod, 8-second exposure. Overcast diffuse light, no harsh shadows. A waterfall blurs into smooth silk-white ribbons over dark wet basalt, the plunge-pool water rendered as soft milky mist; sharp moss-covered rocks and dripping ferns frame the foreground in crisp focus. Natural cool-green color balance, fine grain, the contrast of razor-sharp rock against motion-blurred water.
```


### Forest / woodland interior
- **When:** Inside a forest — tree trunks, canopy light, undergrowth, fog between trunks (no grand vista)
- **Camera:** 24-70mm at 35-50mm, f/8 for moderate depth, ISO 200-400, tripod; telephoto compresses trunk repetition
- **Lighting:** Soft diffuse overcast (avoids harsh dappled blowout), or low sun shafts cutting through morning mist

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 50mm lens at f/8, ISO 200, tripod. Misty autumn morning, soft diffuse overcast light, thin fog hanging between birch trunks and receding into white depth. Damp leaf-litter and fallen golden leaves carpet the foreground in sharp focus; pale vertical trunks recede in repeating layers, bark texture and lichen visible. Muted Kodak Portra color science, low contrast, gentle atmospheric separation, honest woodland detail, no over-saturation.
```


### Minimalist / negative space
- **When:** Stripped-down compositions — a lone tree, single dune, fog, snowfield, calm water with one element
- **Camera:** 50mm or 70-200mm telephoto at f/8-f/11 to isolate; clean simple framing
- **Lighting:** Flat soft fog or overcast, high-key pale tones, or graphic low sun for a single shadow

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 135mm telephoto lens at f/9, ISO 100, tripod. Dense fog flattens the scene into pale grey negative space; a single bare tree stands off-centre, its dark silhouette the only anchor against an empty soft-white field. High-key muted palette, almost monochrome, extremely low contrast, fine grain. Quiet, graphic, minimal — vast emptiness with one small subject, true atmospheric haze rather than a blurred backdrop.
```


### Desert / arid dunes
- **When:** Sand dunes, canyons, badlands, salt flats — texture, ripples, and sculptural light
- **Camera:** 16-35mm wide for sweeping ridgelines, or 70-200mm to compress dune curves; f/11, ISO 100
- **Lighting:** Raking low golden-hour sun across ripples for sharp light/shadow texture; long shadows defining form

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 70-200mm lens at 120mm, f/11, ISO 100, tripod. Late golden-hour sun rakes hard from the left, sculpting the dune crest into a clean light-and-shadow edge, every wind ripple casting a tiny shadow. Curving sand ridgelines layer back into warm haze. Warm amber and deep cool-shadow palette, natural contrast, fine sand-grain texture, a few footprints absent — pristine wind-carved surface, honest desert light not HDR-flattened.
```


### Blue hour / night sky
- **When:** Twilight afterglow, Milky Way, star trails, aurora, or city-lit dusk landscape
- **Camera:** 14-24mm fast wide at f/2.8 for stars, ISO 1600-3200; or f/8 ISO 100 for blue-hour dusk; tripod
- **Lighting:** Deep blue twilight gradient, faint warm glow on the horizon, or Milky Way core over a dark silhouetted land

```text
Shot on a full-frame DSLR with a 20mm lens at f/2.8, ISO 2500, 20-second exposure, tripod. Astrophotography: the Milky Way core arches over a silhouetted mountain ridge, thousands of pinpoint stars with subtle natural color, faint airglow near the horizon. Foreground granite boulders rendered in dim ambient starlight, just-visible texture. Deep navy-to-black sky gradient, restrained noise, realistic limited dynamic range of a real night exposure — not a daylight-bright fake.
```

## img2img / reference-image

For img2img/reference workflows: (1) Supply a real reference photo of the SPECIFIC location or rock/terrain type — gpt-image-2 invents generic peaks otherwise, so a geology/biome anchor matters more than for studio genres. (2) Use a reference primarily to lock composition, horizon placement, and light direction; describe the desired light/season in text since editing a noon source photo into golden hour rarely transfers cleanly. (3) For long-exposure looks, a sharp-water reference still works — just specify '6-stop ND, smooth silk water' in the prompt to override the frozen droplets. (4) When matching a film stock, pass a graded reference frame and name the stock (Velvia/Portra) rather than relying on the image alone. (5) Keep reference influence moderate: high strength copies haze/atmosphere well but can flatten your foreground anchor — restate the foreground texture (wet rock, grass, ripples) in text so it survives. (6) For panoramic/aspect needs, frame the reference at the target ratio; gpt-image-2 respects the supplied crop better than a verbal '16:9 wide panorama'.
