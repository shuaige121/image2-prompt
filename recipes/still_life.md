# Still Life

> **When to use:** Pick this when the subject is an arranged set of inanimate objects on a surface — tabletop product groupings, dark/moody chiaroscuro scenes, overhead flat-lays, or floral bouquets — and the goal is a composed, painterly, photographed-object look rather than a person, landscape, or candid food shot.

## Base recipe

photorealistic still life. [SUBJECT + arrangement: name 3-7 objects and how they sit/lean/overlap on the surface]. Surface and background: [material + color, e.g. weathered oak table, deep charcoal velvet falling into shadow behind]. Shot on a 100mm macro lens at f/8, camera at a low 3/4 angle just above the tabletop, focus pinned on the front object with the rest softening gently. Lighting: [single window light from camera-left / raking side light skimming the surface], soft falloff into deep shadow on the right, warm tungsten-leaning color. Real texture anchors: dust motes in the light beam, fingerprints and water rings on the surface, a single wilting leaf, condensation beading on glass, fine scratches and patina on metal. Muted earthy palette, gentle film grain, natural uneven light. Slightly imperfect arrangement, one object turned off-axis.

## Sub-categories

### Classic tabletop grouping
- **When:** A composed cluster of everyday or product objects on a table, eye-pleasing 3/4 view, balanced but natural — the default 'arranged objects' look.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, f/8, low 3/4 angle just above tabletop height
- **Lighting:** soft window light from camera-left, gentle directional shadow falling to the right

```text
photorealistic still life, a balanced grouping of [3-5 objects] on a weathered oak tabletop, 100mm macro lens at f/8, camera at a low 3/4 angle just above the surface, focus on the front object softening gently toward the back, soft window light from camera-left with a gentle shadow falling right, warm neutral color, real texture: wood grain, faint water rings, a few crumbs and dust, one object turned slightly off-axis, muted palette, fine film grain
```


### Dark / moody chiaroscuro
- **When:** Old-Master, Baroque, single-light drama — a few objects emerging from near-black, strong light-to-shadow contrast, the lit subject as the focal point.
- **Camera:** 100mm, f/5.6, low near-eye-level angle, tight crop
- **Lighting:** single hard side light at 45 degrees (Rembrandt-style), no fill, frame falls to near-black

```text
photorealistic still life in chiaroscuro, [2-3 objects] emerging from deep darkness, single hard light source from camera-left at a 45-degree angle, most of the frame falling into near-black shadow, only the rims and highlights catching light, deep charcoal background, 100mm lens at f/5.6, low eye-level angle, candlelit warm tone, real texture: a glint on tarnished pewter, condensation on a dark wine bottle, dust in the light beam, painterly contrast, fine grain, no fill light
```


### Overhead flat-lay
- **When:** Top-down, perfectly perpendicular 'knolling' or styled-scene flat-lay — items arranged on a surface and shot straight down, even soft light, minimal shadow.
- **Camera:** 50mm, f/8, dead-overhead 90-degree top-down
- **Lighting:** large diffused softbox/window from directly above, even and shadow-minimal

```text
photorealistic overhead flat-lay, [5-7 items] arranged on a [linen / marble / plaster] surface, camera pointed straight down at 90 degrees, 50mm lens at f/8, large soft diffused light from above for even illumination and only faint shadows, clean negative space around the arrangement, real texture: linen weave, a slightly crumpled napkin, a fork nudged off-square, a small spill, soft natural daylight color, subtle grain, controlled imperfection, no harsh shadows
```


### Floral bouquet (Dutch Golden Age)
- **When:** Painterly flower arrangement in a vase against dark ground — lush blooms catching light, the Dutch Masters / vanitas look.
- **Camera:** 100mm, f/4, slightly low angle, vase roughly centered
- **Lighting:** soft directional window light from one side, back of bouquet sinking into dark

```text
photorealistic floral still life in the Dutch Golden Age manner, a loose bouquet of [tulips, roses, peonies] in a [glass / ceramic] vase, against a deep near-black background, soft window light from camera-left catching the front petals while the back of the bouquet falls into shadow, 100mm lens at f/4, slightly low angle, real texture: dewdrops on petals, a curling leaf, a single fallen petal and a small insect on the table, pollen dust, water in the glass vase, rich but muted color, painterly falloff, fine grain
```


### Vanitas / symbolic
- **When:** Memento-mori storytelling arrangement — skull, candle, old books, hourglass, wilting flowers — moody and narrative.
- **Camera:** 100mm, f/5.6, low 3/4 angle, intimate framing
- **Lighting:** single warm candle flame as key light, heavy surrounding shadow

```text
photorealistic vanitas still life, [a skull, a guttering candle, an open leather-bound book, a wilting rose, an hourglass] arranged on a dark wooden table, single warm candlelight as the main source with deep shadow surrounding, 100mm lens at f/5.6, low 3/4 angle, real texture: cracked aged bone, dripping candle wax, foxed yellowed pages, dust, wax pooled on wood, somber muted palette, painterly contrast, fine grain, no studio fill
```


### Bright airy / Scandinavian
- **When:** Light, fresh, high-key still life — pale surfaces, soft shadows, clean modern editorial mood (opposite of moody).
- **Camera:** 85mm, f/4, eye-level 3/4 angle
- **Lighting:** large soft north-window light, low-contrast high-key, long soft shadows

```text
photorealistic still life, [3-4 objects] on a pale plaster or white-washed wood surface, large soft north-facing window light filling the frame, gentle long soft shadows, low contrast and airy, 85mm lens at f/4, eye-level 3/4 angle, real texture: chalky plaster, a soft shadow edge, a few water droplets, natural daylight white balance, restrained pastel palette, subtle grain, calm and unstyled
```


### Macro detail / fragment
- **When:** Extreme close-up on one object's texture — a single fragment, droplets, a surface — abstract and tactile.
- **Camera:** 100mm macro, f/2.8, extreme close-up, razor-thin focus plane
- **Lighting:** low raking side light to maximize surface texture and micro-shadow

```text
photorealistic macro still life, extreme close-up of [a single object / its surface], 100mm macro lens at f/2.8, very shallow depth of field with a sliver in sharp focus melting to soft blur, raking side light skimming across to reveal every texture, real texture: pores, grain, droplets, dust, tiny scratches, fibers, neutral natural color, fine grain, intimate and tactile
```

## img2img / reference-image

When a reference image is supplied, anchor the camera and light from it, not invented specs. Patterns: (1) 'match the lighting direction, shadow softness, and background tone of the reference; keep the same low 3/4 angle' for relighting a new object grouping. (2) For flat-lays, feed an overhead reference and say 'keep the same 90-degree top-down framing and even soft light; restyle the arrangement with [new items]' — gpt-image-2 holds overhead geometry far better from a reference than from text alone. (3) To convert a bright product shot into chiaroscuro, supply the clean shot and prompt 'relight as single hard side light at 45 degrees, drop the background to near-black, keep the object's form and label readable.' (4) For florals, a reference vase/bouquet preserves believable petal and stem structure — describe only the mood/light change. (5) Keep the reference's real-world imperfections (water rings, dust, fingerprints) rather than asking to clean them up — they are what reads as 'photographed, not rendered.' Pull a reference automatically for branded products or specific flower species so shapes/labels stay accurate.
