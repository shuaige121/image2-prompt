# Street / Documentary (candid, reportage, night, motion)

> **When to use:** Pick this when the user wants candid, unposed, real-world human moments — people on streets, reportage/photojournalism, night-city neon, protests, markets, commuters, motion — anything that should look caught, not staged or studio-lit.

## Base recipe

Candid documentary photograph, shot on a handheld 35mm rangefinder with a 35mm lens at f/5.6, zone-focused, 1/500s, [TIME OF DAY + REAL LOCATION: e.g. late-afternoon sidewalk in a busy district]. [SUBJECT doing a specific ordinary action — walking, lighting a cigarette, glancing back, carrying groceries — unaware of the camera]. Off-center reportage framing, slightly tilted horizon, a foreground passer-by partly clipping the edge of the frame, natural available light from [DIRECTION], deep depth of field so the street behind stays legible. Kodak Tri-X / Portra 400 color science, fine 35mm film grain, true skin texture with pores and stray hair, worn clothing, scuffed pavement, ordinary signage and parked cars in the background. Honest exposure with blocked-up shadows and a few clipped highlights, neutral muted color, the imperfect framing of a frame grabbed in a fraction of a second. Real photograph, not posed, not retouched.

[Fill the brackets. Keep it to ONE clear human action + ONE real place + ONE light source. Resist adding adjectives — specify, don't praise.]

## Sub-categories

### Daytime candid street (35mm classic)
- **When:** Default street look — people on a sunny/overcast sidewalk, decisive-moment feel, everything legible.
- **Camera:** 35mm lens, f/8 zone focus, 1/500s, eye-level grabbed-from-the-hip angle
- **Lighting:** flat overcast daylight or hard low sun raking across the street

```text
candid street photograph on a 35mm lens at f/8, zone-focused, 1/500s, midday overcast sidewalk, a man mid-stride glancing sideways, off-center framing with a passer-by clipping the left edge, deep depth of field, soft directional daylight, Portra 400 color, fine grain, real skin pores and creased clothing, scuffed concrete, ordinary shopfronts and parked bikes behind, a frame grabbed in a fraction of a second
```


### Black-and-white reportage (Tri-X / Magnum)
- **When:** Photojournalism, civil-unrest, classic 'decisive moment' monochrome, gritty editorial.
- **Camera:** 28mm or 35mm prime, f/5.6, 1/250s, close working distance
- **Lighting:** hard window/sun light or open shade, high subject contrast

```text
black-and-white documentary photograph, Kodak Tri-X 400 pushed to 1600, 35mm lens at f/5.6, 1/250s available light, a single decisive gesture frozen mid-action, high contrast with deep blacks and visible silver grain, slight motion in a hand, dust and texture, no color, unposed reportage framing with a tilted horizon, honest exposure with crushed shadows, the look of a contact-sheet frame
```


### Night street / neon (Cinestill 800T look)
- **When:** After-dark city, neon signs, rain-wet asphalt, mixed artificial light, cinematic but real.
- **Camera:** 35mm or 50mm fast prime, f/1.4–f/2, 1/125s, ISO 3200
- **Lighting:** mixed neon/sodium/LED city light, no fill, color casts preserved

```text
night street photograph on a 35mm lens at f/2 wide open, 1/125s, ISO 3200, rain-wet city sidewalk, a figure backlit by a neon sign, Cinestill 800T tungsten color science with red halation glowing around the brightest signs, mixed color temperatures left uncorrected — warm sodium-orange streetlamps, green fluorescent shop interiors, blue LED storefronts, heavy luminance grain in the shadows, shallow focus, wet asphalt reflections, a real photograph not corrected for white balance
```


### Motion / panning blur
- **When:** Conveying speed and rush — cyclist, traffic, commuter crowd, runner.
- **Camera:** 35mm lens, 1/30–1/15s, camera tracking the subject horizontally
- **Lighting:** ambient daylight or smeared streetlights at dusk

```text
panned street photograph, slow 1/30s shutter following a moving cyclist, the rider relatively sharp while the background streaks into horizontal motion blur, 35mm lens, deliberate camera movement tracking the subject, daylight, sense of speed and rush hour, fine film grain, blurred neon and headlights smeared across the frame, a real handheld panning shot with slight imperfection in the sharp zone
```


### Hard-flash close-up (Gilden style)
- **When:** In-your-face confrontational portrait of a stranger, harsh popped flash, raw.
- **Camera:** 28mm wide lens, very close working distance, f/8 with flash
- **Lighting:** single harsh frontal flash burst, hard shadows, dark background falloff

```text
confrontational candid close-up on a 28mm lens, harsh direct on-camera flash fired at close range, every facial pore wrinkle and stray hair starkly lit, hard black drop-shadow thrown onto the wall behind, deep falloff into darkness at the edges, the subject caught mid-expression and partly clipped by the frame, gritty unflattering realism, slight overexposure on the nearest skin, a raw flash street photograph
```


### Environmental / market documentary
- **When:** Vendors, workers, daily-life scenes where the place matters as much as the person.
- **Camera:** 35mm lens, f/4–f/5.6, mid distance, slightly low candid angle
- **Lighting:** side window light, open shade, or shaft of sun into the stall

```text
environmental documentary photograph of a market vendor at a stall, 35mm lens at f/4, available light, the person working amid real clutter — produce crates, handwritten price signs, plastic bags, worn surfaces — context-rich framing keeping the whole scene legible, natural daylight from one side, Portra 400 muted color, true skin and fabric texture, unposed mid-task moment, a working reportage frame
```


### Crowd / protest from the hip
- **When:** Dense crowds, demonstrations, festivals — wide, immersive, multiple subjects.
- **Camera:** 28mm wide lens, f/8 deep DOF, low hip-level angle, fast shutter 1/500s
- **Lighting:** open daylight, hard shadows from overhead sun

```text
wide candid documentary photograph from inside a dense crowd, 28mm lens at f/8, shot from the hip, multiple people at different distances all caught mid-motion, foreground head clipping the frame edge, slight Dutch tilt, available daylight, raised hands and signs, layered depth, fine grain, the chaotic imperfect framing of a frame grabbed while moving through people, real reportage
```

## img2img / reference-image

Street/documentary is the genre where reference images matter most, because the model otherwise drifts toward clean, posed, studio-lit people. Patterns: (1) Use a reference for the LIGHT and GRAIN, not the subject — pass a real night-street or Tri-X frame and prompt 'match the film grain, color cast, and harsh mixed lighting of the reference; keep my subject and pose.' (2) Label each input by role to stop drift: 'Image 1 = subject/pose to keep; Image 2 = lighting+grain reference to copy; Image 3 = the street/background.' (3) When editing a too-clean AI face toward realism, instruct 'preserve identity and pose, add real skin pores, stray hair, uneven skin tone, and 35mm film grain; do NOT smooth or beautify.' (4) For night/neon, an 800T reference is the fastest way to get correct halation and uncorrected color casts that words alone under-deliver. (5) State explicitly what must NOT change (identity, framing, the off-center crop) — the model will otherwise 'fix' your deliberate imperfect framing into centered, balanced composition. (6) Keep input fidelity high when the candid likeness of a specific person must survive a background/lighting change.
