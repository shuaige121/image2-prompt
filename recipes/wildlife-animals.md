# Wildlife & Animals

> **When to use:** Pick this when the subject is a live animal — wild mammals/birds shot at distance, a pet portrait, birds in flight or on a perch, or a close-up insect/macro — and the user wants a believable photograph, not an illustration or render.

## Base recipe

Write it as a shot brief, not a wish list. Fill these slots in order:

[Animal + species + exact behavior/pose] photographed on a [named telephoto/macro lens, e.g. 600mm f/4 telephoto] at [aperture] from [low / eye-level / ground perspective], [framing: tight head-and-shoulders portrait | full-body in habitat | wing-spread in flight]. Subject in sharp focus on the [near eye with a small catchlight], background a [soft creamy out-of-focus wash of the real habitat] from the long lens and wide aperture. Lit by [direction + quality of natural light: low golden-hour side light / soft overcast / backlit rim light through fur or feathers]. Real-texture anchors: [individual fur strands / barbs and shafts of feathers / wet nose / scales / compound-eye facets / dewdrops]. Color science: natural daylight white balance, muted earthy field tones, slight warmth, restrained contrast. Frame so the animal has room to look into; uneven natural background, a few out-of-focus grass blades or twigs crossing the frame. Candid wildlife moment, shot handheld on a real camera, not posed or composited.

RULES: name ONE real lens and aperture; keep depth of field shallow so the habitat melts but the eye stays tack-sharp; always put a catchlight in the eye; let the background be the actual environment blurred, never a seamless studio sweep; allow natural imperfection (a stray blade of grass, slight motion in a wingtip, dirt on paws). Do not over-saturate — wildlife reads as fake the instant colors go neon.

## Sub-categories

### Telephoto big-game / mammal portrait
- **When:** Lions, foxes, deer, bears, elephants — a single wild mammal isolated against blurred habitat
- **Camera:** 600mm f/4 super-telephoto (or 400mm f/2.8), aperture f/4, shot from a low ground-level / eye-to-eye angle
- **Lighting:** low golden-hour side light raking across the fur, long warm shadows

```text
a red fox standing alert in dry winter grass, photographed on a 600mm f/4 telephoto at f/4 from a low eye-level angle on the ground, tight three-quarter body framing, the near eye tack-sharp with a tiny catchlight, individual guard hairs and the wet black nose visible, background an extremely soft creamy wash of golden grassland thrown far out of focus by the long lens, low golden-hour side light raking across the fur with long warm shadows, natural daylight white balance and muted earthy field tones, a couple of out-of-focus grass blades crossing the foreground, candid wildlife moment shot handheld on a real camera
```


### Environmental / habitat animalscape
- **When:** User wants the animal small-in-frame showing its landscape and story, not a tight portrait
- **Camera:** 300mm f/2.8 or 100-400mm zoom at the wide end / 70mm, aperture f/8 for more habitat in focus
- **Lighting:** soft directional dawn light, atmospheric haze, layered depth

```text
a lone elephant walking across a wide savanna at dawn, photographed on a 100-400mm telephoto at 200mm and f/8, animal placed small in the lower third of the frame with the herd's environment dominating, dust and low golden haze catching the backlight, layered receding grassland and a distant tree line softly compressed by the long lens, natural daylight color with muted dusty earth tones and gentle contrast, the animal sharp against soft atmospheric background, a quiet documentary wildlife moment on a real camera
```


### Bird in flight
- **When:** Wings spread, raptor/heron/duck mid-air, frozen action
- **Camera:** 500mm f/5.6 or 600mm f/4, aperture f/5.6, very fast shutter ~1/3200s to freeze the wings
- **Lighting:** bright open daylight or low backlight glowing through the wing feathers

```text
a great blue heron in flight with wings fully spread, photographed on a 600mm f/4 telephoto at f/5.6 and a very fast 1/3200s shutter that freezes every feather, panning with the bird against a soft out-of-focus sky, sharp focus on the eye and head with individual flight-feather barbs and shafts visible, low afternoon sun rim-lighting the trailing wing edge so the feathers glow translucent, natural daylight white balance, clean uncluttered background, crisp candid wildlife action frame on a real camera
```


### Bird on a perch / songbird portrait
- **When:** Static bird on a branch, jewel-like detail, classic frame-filling bird portrait
- **Camera:** 500mm f/4 or 400mm f/5.6, aperture f/5.6, eye-level with the perch
- **Lighting:** soft overcast diffused light or gentle early-morning sun, no harsh shadows

```text
a male European robin perched on a moss-covered twig, photographed on a 500mm f/4 telephoto at f/5.6 at eye level with the perch, the bird filling the right side of the frame looking into the open space, the near eye razor-sharp with a small catchlight, fine feather texture and tiny beak highlight rendered crisply, background a smooth creamy green bokeh wash of distant foliage from the long lens, soft overcast diffused morning light with no harsh shadows, natural muted color, an out-of-focus lichen-covered branch leading in, intimate wildlife portrait on a real camera
```


### Pet portrait (dog / cat)
- **When:** A specific companion animal, warm and characterful, indoor or backyard
- **Camera:** 85mm f/1.8 portrait lens (or 70-200mm at 135mm), aperture f/2, slightly below the pet's eye line
- **Lighting:** soft north-facing window light at 45 degrees, large soft catchlight in the eyes

```text
a golden retriever lying on a wooden floor looking up, photographed on an 85mm f/1.8 portrait lens at f/2 from slightly below the dog's eye line, tight head-and-shoulders framing, both eyes warm and sharp with a large soft window-light catchlight, individual fur strands and the moist nose and whiskers visible, background of the living room melted into a gentle out-of-focus blur, soft north-facing window light coming from 45 degrees with no flash, natural warm indoor white balance and gentle contrast, relaxed candid pet moment on a real camera
```


### Macro insect / small creature
- **When:** Butterfly, bee, dragonfly, beetle, spider — extreme close-up, compound-eye and texture detail
- **Camera:** 100mm f/2.8 macro lens at 1:1 magnification, aperture f/11-f/16 for usable depth of field, focus-stacked sharpness
- **Lighting:** diffused twin-flash acting like a small softbox, soft even wraparound light

```text
a dragonfly resting on a reed at dawn, photographed on a 100mm f/2.8 macro lens at 1:1 magnification and f/13, extreme close-up filling the frame, razor-sharp from the multi-faceted compound eyes through the veined translucent wings, dew droplets clinging to the body and wing membranes, soft diffused twin-flash lighting wrapping evenly around the insect like a small softbox, smooth dark green out-of-focus background, natural color with crisp micro-texture on the exoskeleton, fine detailed nature macro on a real camera
```


### Underwater / wet-environment wildlife
- **When:** Seal, otter, turtle, fish, frog at or under the waterline
- **Camera:** wide or mid telephoto behind underwater housing, aperture f/8, half-over-half split or fully submerged
- **Lighting:** dappled sunlight shafts filtering down through water, cool blue-green ambient

```text
a sea turtle gliding just below the surface, photographed on a 16-35mm wide lens in an underwater housing at f/8, slightly low angle looking up toward the bright surface, the turtle sharp with light shafts of dappled sunlight streaming down through the water, fine detail on the shell scutes and flippers, suspended particles catching the light, cool blue-green ambient water color with natural underwater white balance, soft falloff into deeper blue behind, authentic dive-shot wildlife frame on a real camera
```


### Snow / cold-weather wildlife
- **When:** Animal in snow, frosty breath, winter coat — high-key bright field
- **Camera:** 600mm f/4 telephoto, aperture f/4, exposed +1 stop so snow stays white not grey
- **Lighting:** flat soft overcast or low side sun, breath fog and frost on fur

```text
a snow monkey sitting in a steaming hot spring during a snowfall, photographed on a 600mm f/4 telephoto at f/4, exposure pushed slightly brighter so the snow reads clean white, tight portrait with frost crystals on the fur and visible warm breath fog, the near eye sharp with a catchlight, falling snowflakes rendered as soft out-of-focus dots from the long lens, flat soft overcast winter light, cool neutral white balance with a touch of warmth on the face, quiet contemplative wildlife moment on a real camera
```

## img2img / reference-image

Reference images steer wildlife better than any adjective for two things: exact species/markings and the real bokeh look of a long lens. Patterns that work: (1) Identity/species lock — pass a photo of the actual animal and write "Image 1: reference of this specific [breed/species] — keep its exact coat pattern, markings, ear shape, eye color and body proportions; do not change the animal's identity," then describe only the new pose/light/background. Critical for a user's own pet or a recognizable individual. (2) Bokeh/lens reference — pass a real telephoto wildlife shot as a style reference: "Image 2: match this shallow depth of field, creamy background falloff and natural field color — apply only the look, not the subject." (3) Habitat plate — give a landscape photo as the background and composite the animal in at correct scale and matching light direction. (4) Pose transfer — sketch or photo of the desired pose plus a separate species reference; state which image governs identity vs. pose. Always repeat the preserve list ("keep the markings, keep the eye color") on every edit iteration to stop drift, and re-state the catchlight and sharp-eye requirement since edits tend to soften eyes. For macro, a reference of real diffused-flash lighting prevents the model from flattening the subject with even fill.
